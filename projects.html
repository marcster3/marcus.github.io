Breakfast for Two

Written and Directed by Marcus Wright

When someone contemplates death, their friend shows them how much they care

Synopsis

Charlie is feeling depressed and is thinking about death. Charlie and Sam have been in the same situation before and Sam knows exactly what to say to Charlie to get her out of her rut. Sam makes jokes to Charlie and talks about what tomorrow morning would look like without her. Charlie is unable to resist the temptation to laugh and smile. Sam talks about his own struggle with mental health to make Charlie feel more normal. Charlie begins to be able to feel better about herself, feeling happier and knowing someone truly cares about her.

Director's Statement

A friend contemplating suicide is ont of the most terrifying realities to face in friendship. Being careful about what you say and how you say it is paramount in those moments. It takes empathy, love, and understanding while also trying to make that person feel normal and remind them how much you care about them. I am very inspired by Ingmar Bergmanâ€™s films where conversations on topics of this severity are dealt with in the most plain and direct way while also feeling extremely human. His focus on people as being loved and loving while being flawed is something I wanted to explore. They both have admiration and care for each other that is clearly beyond what most normal friendships would be. Through this understanding Sam is able to help Charlie and show how much he cares about him.

Courser

Designed and Programmed by Marcus Wright

An action adventure game playing as a robot dog designed by a young boy to get medicine for his sick mom.

Game Description

In a post apocalyptic world a boy and his mother have sought shelter outside of the govement controlled city. After his mother gets sick the boy builds a robot to fetch the medicine she needs to improve. Playing as the robot you must navigate your way through an overoxygenated enviroment that has effected both plants an wildlife and the occasional survailence of the goverment.

Additional Notes

Courser is programmed in C# using the Unity engine. The 3D Design is done in Blender and Rhino 7.
